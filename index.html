My Guiding Principles
  • Solve the root-cause, not the symptom Sometimes it's easy to apply a quick fix without understanding why a bug appeared. Hotfixes are fine—but the underlying cause must always be uncovered, documented, and addressed.
  • Automate only when it earns its keep I've learned when automation amplifies clarity and when it simply adds machinery. Tools should serve people, not the other way around.
  • Data and DRY One of the most significant problems I've seen in software is bad software patterns that require what is known as "shotgun surgery" to maintain. It's better for data to drive logic.
